About

The short version, and the honest one.

I'm a technical professional who spends his days helping enterprise customers navigate AI platforms and his nights reading Dostoevsky, building homelab infrastructure, learning to draw from classical casts, and 3D printing things for the apartment.

I call this the Renaissance Craftsman approach: the belief that cross-domain exploration isn't scattered focus; it's how pattern recognition works. The best ideas come from connecting things that aren't supposed to be connected. A medieval guild system teaches you something about modern skill acquisition. A Victorian knowledge management practice informs how you build a digital second brain.

I document this journey across two YouTube channels, a growing library of 350+ books spanning 50 domains, and a personal knowledge system called the Scriptorium that integrates analog notebooks, AI collaboration, and a digital vault.

I'm not a productivity influencer. I'm someone trying to stay human in an increasingly automated world, and sharing what I find along the way.
