About

The short version, and the honest one.

I'm CK. By day, I'm an AI platform specialist helping enterprise customers navigate complex technical systems. By night, I'm reading Dostoevsky, building homelab infrastructure on Linux, learning to draw from classical casts, 3D printing things for the apartment, and trying to figure out how medieval guild systems can teach us about modern skill acquisition.

I call this the Renaissance Craftsman approach. Not because I'm trying to be Leonardo da Vinci. Because I genuinely believe that the best ideas come from connecting things that aren't supposed to be connected. A Victorian knowledge management practice informs how you build a digital second brain. A 15th-century workshop layout teaches you about creative environments. An 800-year-old apprenticeship model turns out to be better for ADHD brains than any modern productivity system.

I'm not a productivity influencer. I'm not optimizing my morning routine. I'm someone who got obsessed with too many things, spent years thinking that was a problem, and eventually realized it was the whole point.

I document this journey across two YouTube channels, a growing library of 350+ books spanning 50+ domains, and a personal knowledge system called the Scriptorium that integrates analog notebooks, AI collaboration, and a digital vault. If any of that sounds interesting, you're in the right place.

The Scriptorium

A three-layer system for thinking, creating, and learning.

The Scriptorium is my personal knowledge system, named after the medieval writing rooms where monks preserved and created knowledge. It's not a productivity hack. It's an architecture for how I actually think: analog exploration first, AI collaboration second, digital archive third. Each layer serves a different cognitive function. Together, they form something I haven't found anywhere else.

I

Scholar's Manuscript

Analog notebooks. Kokuyo Systemic cover with three Midori MD inserts: a weekly scheduler, a blank thinking canvas for visual frameworks, and a dotted commonplace book. The hand thinks differently than the keyboard.

II

Scholar's Brain

AI collaboration as cognitive extension. Claude as Strategic Cortex for deep synthesis and decision-making. Gemini as Creative Cortex for real-time research and aesthetic direction. Two minds, different strengths.

III

Scholar's Web

Obsidian vault with a nine-space architecture: Mind Dump, Library, Domains, Sparks, Projects, Chronicle, Creations, Canon, and Completed. 500+ notes. The permanent record of everything that survives the other two layers.
